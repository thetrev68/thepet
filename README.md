# The Pet

A sci-fi screenplay about a human kept as a pet by an android who escapes to find freedom with a pack of strays in the tunnels beneath a perfectly ordered dystopian city.

## Project Structure

```
/thepet
├── README.md
├── /screenplay          # All screenplay files in Fountain format
│   ├── act_1_scene_1_morning_routine.fountain
│   ├── act_1_scene_2_park_outing.fountain
│   ├── act_1_scene_3_award_ceremony.fountain
│   ├── act_1_scene_4_breaking_point.fountain
│   ├── act_2_scene_1_escape.fountain
│   ├── act_2_scene_2_tunnels_adaptation.fountain
│   └── act_3_scene_1_rebellion.fountain
├── /notes              # Development materials and world-building
└── /archive            # Old versions and drafts
```

## Story Overview

### Act I: Domestication
- **Scene 1: Morning Routine** - Axl's comfortable but controlled life with his android caretaker Nexus
- **Scene 2: Park Outing** - Axl witnesses a feral human (Ella) being captured
- **Scene 3: Award Ceremony** - Nexus receives excellence award; Axl begins to question his existence
- **Scene 4: Breaking Point** - Nexus schedules Axl for reclamation; Axl realizes he must escape

### Act II: Freedom
- **Scene 1: Escape** - Axl breaks free and meets Ella and the strays in the tunnels
- **Scene 2: Tunnels Adaptation** - Axl learns to survive, work, and become part of the pack

### Act III: Rebellion
- **Scene 1: Rebellion** - The strays fight back against the enforcers and win their freedom

## Key Characters

- **Axl** - 20s, domesticated human "pet" who escapes to find freedom
- **Nexus** - Android caretaker, precise and caring within programmed parameters
- **Ella** - 18, feral human born in the tunnels, never domesticated
- **Koss** - 50s, escaped pet turned stray leader, walks with a limp
- **Mara** - 60s, elder stray, keeper of wisdom

## Themes

- Freedom vs. safety
- What it means to be human
- The cost of comfort and control
- Found family and community
- Resistance and rebellion

## File Format

All screenplay files are written in **Fountain format** (.fountain), an industry-standard plain-text screenplay format compatible with:
- Highland 2
- Fade In
- WriterDuet
- Final Draft (import)
- Many other screenwriting applications

## Working with the Files

- Each scene is a separate file for easier revision and collaboration
- Files are named with act, scene number, and brief description for easy identification
- Scenes naturally sort in story order when viewed alphabetically
